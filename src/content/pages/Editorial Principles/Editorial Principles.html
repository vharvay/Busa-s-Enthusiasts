Editorial Principles

Our bilingual digital edition of An Account of a Voyage up the River de la Plata, and thence over land to Peru, by Mons. Acarete du Biscay, follows a diplomatic approach that focuses on preserving the original text as it was written, with its old-fashioned spelling, formatting, and typos, all highlighted across the text, so the reader can study both the text itself and the context of its creation, which we believe has paratextual elements that are worth pointing out as they help draw comparisons between both editions, in terms of graphical conventions and other printing aspects that vary dramatically when you factor in the time gap between them.

At the same time, to provide a modernize re-reading we have encode the text following a series of tags that will help the reader contextualize the text and the author himself, by highlighting the way the author positions himself in relation with others, and how his interactions with different people and places impact directly on the choices of words throughout the text. Finally, our edition also includes extra geographical data of the places that are mentioned in the text by linking them to the information provided by the World Historical Gazetteer website.

For further information, here is the list of the TEI tags used in this edition and a brief explanation of each one:

  • pb/ and lb/

    We use these tags in order to mark the page breaks and line breaks as they appear in the original text in both editions. This allows to more easily identify other elements that are also present in the original texts, such as running headers, catch words and page numbers.

  • fw type="head"

    This tag is used to identify all the running headers in both editions, which contain the page numbers and other paratextual elements.

  • fw type="catch"

    As we wanted the reader to access the original text and be able to identify the elements and graphical conventions that were specific to that particular context in which the edition took place, we identify the catch words with this tag and we coloured them in blue, so as to also isolate these words from the rest of the text, and keep the reading going smoothly.

  • orig

    As noted before, this edition does not pursue a normalization of the text and what might be considered up for correction in a modern edition, but it rather follows the original text and the original words as they were written. This element appears coloured in yellow.

  • sic

    The use of this tag is to only highlight (not offer a correction or modernization) what might be considered an incorrect or inexact word, a typo, by today’s standards regarding the use of both languages. This element appears coloured in green.

  • name type="place"

  • name type="placeGeog"

  • name type="person"

  • name type="personRoleName"

  • rs type="caste"

  • rs type="otherness"

    We decided on this element and we named it “otherness” as a way of kicking off a discussion around the fact that the author is always defining himself and his journey in relation (or opposition) to others. The words he chooses to name other people are the most palpable evidence of the context of his journey and its “difficult heritage”, which revolve around the idea of otherness, and the impact it has on the author and his choices of words.

  • rs type="ReligOrder"

  • rs type="people"

  • measure type="currency"

  • note

    We use this tag to provide a digital version of the footnotes that appear in the original version.

  • pb/ y lb/

    Usamos estas etiquetas para marcar los saltos de página y los saltos de línea tal como aparecen en el texto original en ambas ediciones. Esto permite identificar más fácilmente otros elementos que también están presentes en los textos originales, como encabezados, palabras clave y números de página.

  • fw type="head"

    Esta etiqueta se utiliza para identificar todos los encabezados continuos en ambas ediciones, que contienen los números de página y otros elementos paratextuales.

  • fw type="catch"

    Como queríamos que el lector accediera al texto original y pudiera identificar los elementos y convenciones gráficas que eran específicos de ese contexto particular en el que se llevó a cabo la edición, identificamos las palabras clave con esta etiqueta y las coloreamos en azul, para aislar también estas palabras del resto del texto y mantener la lectura fluida.

  • orig

    Como se señaló anteriormente, esta edición no persigue una normalización del texto y lo que podría considerarse corregido en una edición moderna, sino que sigue el texto original y las palabras originales tal como fueron escritas. Este elemento aparece coloreado en amarillo.

  • sic

    El uso de esta etiqueta es solo para resaltar (no ofrecer una corrección o modernización) lo que podría considerarse una palabra incorrecta o inexacta, un error tipográfico, según los estándares actuales con respecto al uso de ambos idiomas. Este elemento aparece coloreado en verde.
